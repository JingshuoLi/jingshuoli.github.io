Spirit Walking — Quadruped Loco-Manipulation with MPPI

Quadruped Locomotion / Optimal Control

Developing a hierarchical loco-manipulation framework for the Spirit 40 quadruped robot that combines reinforcement learning-based locomotion control with Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) planning for autonomous box-pushing tasks.

The system integrates a PPO-trained low-level locomotion policy with a high-level MPPI planner that optimizes body velocity commands in parallel GPU-based simulation using Genesis. The framework also includes a body-fixed single-leg manipulation mode that directly optimizes swing-leg joint trajectories for contact-rich interaction tasks.

Built with PyTorch, Genesis simulator, and rsl-rl, with support for large-scale parallel trajectory rollouts and learned whole-body control.

TrussSpirit — Quadruped Locomotion Controller for Structured Environments

Quadruped Locomotion / Control Systems
NASA TRUSS Project

Developed a custom locomotion controller for the Spirit 40 quadruped robot as part of a joint research effort between the University of Pennsylvania and the University of Southern California, targeting stable locomotion on space truss structures.

Extended the Ghost Robotics Spirit SDK with TrussSpirit, a custom robot class implementing robust state estimation for non-flat, structured environments. A pair-frame-based world frame estimator computes the ground contact plane from all six combinations of stance foot pairs, providing yaw-robust body pose tracking even during single-leg swing phases. CoM position and velocity are estimated via weighted foot averaging or a convex-hull centroid approach.

The stance controller supports external body force feedforward for payload-aware balancing, with force estimates derived from per-leg Jacobian-transpose inversion of joint torque readings. High-level velocity and force commands are streamed in real time over MAVLink between the onboard Jetson TX2 and the Spirit mainboard. Implemented in C++ with Eigen, deployed and tested on the Spirit 40 hardware at the LASSIE Lab at Penn.

Coupled Jet Coordination and Physical Arrangement in Salp-Inspired Multi-Robot Swimming

Bio-inspired Robotics / Fluid Dynamics

Published in Bioinspiration & Biomimetics, 2025.

Contributed to experimental flow characterization and analysis for a salp-inspired multi-robot swimming system developed at Sung Robotics Lab. My work focused on particle image velocimetry (PIV) experiments, including flow measurement, vortex analysis, and data processing to support the free-swimming performance studies of coordinated underwater robotic systems.

The project investigated how jet coordination and physical arrangement influence propulsion efficiency and locomotion performance in modular soft underwater robots inspired by biological salps.
